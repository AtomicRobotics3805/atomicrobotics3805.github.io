Our Team

3805 Atomic Robotics is a group of students participating in the yearly robotics competition called FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC). FTC was founded by Dean Kamen in 1989 with the hopes of introducing a wide variety of students to the STEM fields in a fun and interactive way. In addition to programming and engineering skills, our team is involved in outreach and community support, such as park clean-ups, virtual collaborations, and getting young people excited about STEM opportunities.

Game Manual

Game Manual 3805 is an online resource library containing FTC related information that our team has found valuable over our past seasons. It includes detailed entries about engineering process, in-depth tutorials on mechanisms our team has used, tips on fundraising and outreach, step-by-step instructions and tips for programming with Kotlin, and more!

Sponsorships

Atomic Robotics uses sponsorship money to buy essential parts to build our robot such as sensors, structural components, and motion systems, such as motors and servos, to provide our team with valuable engineering experience! Sponsorship money also helps with travel expenses so we can ensure everyone who wants to is able to participate in and attend competitions.
